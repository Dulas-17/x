What are astronomical seasons?

Astronomical seasons are the four seasons namely:Summer,Autum,Winter and Spring that are defined by the Earth's position in its obirt around the sun.

Astronomical Summer

Astronomical Summer is one of the four seasons,characterized by the warmest temperatures of the year.It is a time when days are longest,and nights are shortest;making it the brightest season.Northern hemisphere summer begins around june 21st/22nd lasting until around september 22nd/23rd and in the southern hemisphere it begins around december 21st/22nd and ends around murch 21st/22nd.

Astronomical Autum

Astronomical Autum,also known as fall in some regions.This season is characterized by the vibrant transformation of foliage,with leaves changing colors to shades of red,orange,yellow and brown before eventually falling to the ground.In the northern hemisphere autum begins around september 22nd/23rd lasting until around december 21st/22nd and in the southern hemisphere begins march 20th/22nd and ends around june 20th/21st.

Astronomical Winter

Astronomical Winter is the coldest season of the year,characterized by shorter days and long nights.In the northern hemisphere ,winter begins around december 21st/22nd and ending around march 20th/21st and in the southern hemisphere it begins around june 20th/21st and ends around september 22nd/23rd.despite the coldness winter brings its own beauty and sense of peacefulness,as nature rests and prepares for renewal of spring.

Astronomical Spring

Astronomical Spring is a season of renewal,growth,and transformation;as the world awakens from dormancy of winter.In the northern hemisphere spring typically begins around march 20th/21st and ends around june 20th/21st and in the southern hemisphere spring typically begins around september 22nd/23rd and ends around december 21st/22nd.During spring trees and plants begin to bud and bloom,covering the landscape in vibrant colors.
